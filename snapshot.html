Trampin Day in Review

Where the work is right now — top-paying locals, the biggest boards, and the projects driving demand, pulled live from union dispatch.

Trampin the IBEW Indoor Locals — Tuesday, July 7, 2026

LU-683 Columbus, OH is running clean at $73.42/hr across multiple standing calls—Mid City's got 20 Inside JW slots at various locations, while ESI is pushing the Cologix Data Center in Lewis Center. These are your top-dollar bread-and-butter runs in the Midwest right now, and with only 115 hands chasing 25 calls, Book 1's getting thin fast.

LU-494 Milwaukee, WI has the Port Washington Data Center reposted *hard*—MJ Electric and Gurtz are both hunting, offering JW wage plus $10/hr ($64.75+) for 50–60 hour weeks. You'll need a valid WI license and clean drug screen, but 36 calls against 182 hands means real velocity. Wil-Surge is also grabbing service work at scale-plus.

LU-237 Niagara Falls, NY owns the Lake Mariner Data play right now. Ferguson and O'Connell are both locked in at $48.20/hr, per diem available, first shift 7/10s with onboarding Mon–Tue–Thu (Ferguson) or Monday only (O'Connell). Only 8 calls total but 104 hands means you move fast or you miss it.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND is the sleeper: Parsons Electric's Polaris Forge Data Center pays $44.80/hr base + 31.4% shift differential on 2nd shift, plus $100/day non-taxed per diem and incentive pay. That math clears $65+/hr effective. Hunt Electric's got Grand Forks Air Base work at the same rate plus Friday OT. Book's only 102 deep on 24 calls.

LU-613 Atlanta, GA has 35 live calls but the pay's softer—Inglett & Stubbs and Miller hovering $40–$42.18/hr on hospital and QTS work. Four-tens dominate; saturation's high at 204 hands per 35 calls.

LU-583 El Paso, TX shows the data center wage split: Rosendin at $40 + $15 incentive for 60-hour weeks (OT double time), while Summit, Cupertino, and B&D are all $55/hr but demand NM State JIW licenses and OSHA 10.

Verdict: Data centers own the board. Wisconsin scales (Milwaukee, Racine, Janesville) and Columbus are where the per-hour money sits, but Grand Forks' 31.4% shift diff plus tax-free per diem is the true earner if you can stomach cold and second shift.

Trampin the IBEW Lineman Locals — Tuesday, July 7, 2026

LU-583 El Paso, TX is running hot with $55.00/hr scale and zero book depth — 10 calls, only 92 hands total. Summit Line, Cupertino Electric, and B&D Industries are all hiring Inside JWs on projects requiring NM State JIW License EE98J and OSHA 10. Rosendin's got 37 slots at $40 + $15 incentive for hitting 90% of 60 hrs/week, double-time OT. This is quick-turn work.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND has $44.80/hr with serious per diem: Parsons Electric is running 56 Inside JWs on the Polaris Forge Data Center (2nd shift, +31.4% shift differential on top of $100/day non-taxed per diem). Hunt Electric's got another 10 on the Air Force base with additional $100/day for ND license holders. Book is deep at 102 hands, but the shift premiums and incentive pay ($100–$150/day M-F/Sat-Sun) make the real wage north of $50/hr. Requires Real ID or passport.

LU-222 Orlando, FL is a specialty play: $175.00/hr barehand certified work on transmission, $150/hr substation tech roles with $150–$175/day per diem. Only 3 calls and 7 hands, but Service Electric's Newberry transmission gig and New River's Palatka substation rebuild are premium short calls. Tight board.

LU-1393 Indianapolis, IN shows $64.66/hr lineman and $51.79–$56.66/hr substation tech with $75–$125/day per diem, 25 calls on 32 hands. ARC American (Wanatah 34kv/12.5kv rebuild), Kent Power (Elkhart substation), and Intren (brownfield upgrade) all need CDL A. Real depth, real work.

LU-291 Boise, ID pairs $45.33–$65.33/hr with $170/day per diem (7 days if eligible) on the Micron PLA and St. Luke's Tower. Only 9 calls for 45 hands — book's clearing.

Data-center projects and utility infrastructure rebuilds are absorbing the bulk of available hands; transmission and substation work still commands the premium rate.
