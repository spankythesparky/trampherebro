Trampin Day in Review

Where the work is right now — top-paying locals, the biggest boards, and the projects driving demand, pulled live from union dispatch.

Trampin the IBEW Indoor Locals — Wednesday, July 15, 2026

LU-481 Indianapolis, IN is running hot with 44 calls and $54.75/hr scale. Google's Mooresville data center (Ermco, 25 hands) and Meta's Lebanon facility (Meade Electric, 20 hands at $53.80/hr, 5-12s Mon-Fri plus Saturday) are both pulling steady. IU Hospital adds another 21 calls split between Industrial Electric and Electric Plus. This is the Midwest's strongest board right now — pure new construction work, 10% premiums on some Meta slots, and no book depth issues.

LU-237 Niagara Falls, NY has 115 hands chasing 8 calls at $48.20/hr, but Lake Mariner Data Center is the prize: 90 hands between Ferguson and O'Connell Electric alone, first and second shifts, per diem available. Amazon's also running 25 calls on a 5-10s + 1-8 schedule. Per diem here makes the math work even on mid-tier scale.

LU-613 Atlanta, GA — 40 calls, $48.00/hr top scale. Inglett & Stubbs dominates with two standing calls (hospital expansion and QTS Fayette) plus the cable splicer work at Miller (requires 3 years documented). Beltline's Plant Yates run in Newnan hits $42.18/hr but it's heavy industrial SCMMA agreement, 5-10s, 6:30am start.

LU-1105 Newark, OH is grinding with 32 calls across multiple contractors. Superior Group's Conesville plant (30 hands, 4-10s + Saturday), Lighthouse Electric's New Albany (per diem $5.75 over scale, 5-12s + 2-8s, 45-hour minimums), and ESI's newer Albany work ($2.01/hr over dayshift) keep the board moving.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND — 27 calls at $44.80/hr base plus $100/day non-taxed per diem (Mon-Fri) and $150 Sat/Sun makes the real comp closer to $62/hr. Parsons Electric's Polaris Forge Data Center is eating both their major calls; GPS radius is 100+ miles but the incentive pay structure is unbeatable.

Data center work and infrastructure build-out are vacuuming hands north and east — Milwaukee's Port Washington play alone has 65 calls, and Polaris Forge is rewriting the per diem game in rural markets.

Trampin the IBEW Lineman Locals — Wednesday, July 15, 2026

LU-71 Columbus, OH is printing money right now — 41 calls across four contractors, $61.44 top scale, and New River Elect. Corp is running dual distro jobs: 36 JW's on 6-10's in Columbus with $125/night per diem and a $100 bonus, plus 9 more across Southern Ohio on 4-10's optional weekends. JJM Power and Thayer Power are filling crew slots with make-ready and reconductor work. Book depth is solid at 126 hands needed.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND has 27 calls but don't sleep on the comp package — Polaris Forge Data Center in Harwood is eating up most of the board with $44.80/hr plus $100/day non-taxed per diem Mon-Fri, $150 weekends, and 6-10's means real money on the incentive side. Parsons Electric alone has 43 Inside JW slots, plus Hunt Electric's Grand Forks Air Base gig adds $100/day with ND license on top of base per diem.

LU-84 Newnan, GA — 19 calls, $46.80 scale, and W2 Energy Services is reconducuring Social Circle with 25 JW's, $125/day per diem, while Fairway Electric's grid call in Jefferson is ramping to 6-10's and 7-10's with $100/day. 81 hands on the board means movement.

LU-291 Boise, ID has the Micron fab job locked in across four contractors — $45.33/hr but $170/day x7 per diem if eligible makes it $1,190/week cushion on a 4-10 PLA build. VECA, OEG, and Lea are all hiring Inside JW's on the same project with the 60-60-50 schedule.

LU-1393 Indianapolis, IN — 25 calls, $67.18 top scale. ARC American's Wanatah rebuild at $64.66 and AMPP's AEP distribution work at $56.66 with $100/day per diem keep the board moving.

LU-1547 Anchorage, AK has 50 calls but thin specialization — mostly laborer, tree trimmer, and telecom work below $56/hr despite the $76.59 scale; data-center work is pulling Inside JW's south to Grand Forks and Greensboro instead.

Trampin the UA Locals — Wednesday, July 15, 2026

LU-140 Salt Lake City, UT is the board heavyweight this week—28 calls hunting 105 hands means Book 1 is moving fast. Industrial maintenance and fab work is eating dispatch right now; pipeline's quiet, and data-center build is slowing refinement-heavy locals instead.
