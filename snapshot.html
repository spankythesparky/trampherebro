Trampin Day in Review

Where the work is right now — top-paying locals, the biggest boards, and the projects driving demand, pulled live from union dispatch.

Trampin the IBEW Indoor Locals — Monday, July 13, 2026

LU-481 Indianapolis, IN is running hot with 43 calls and 191 hands on the board. Meade Electric's Meta Data Center gig pays $59.18/hr (10% over the $54.75 scale) for 5-12's Mon-Fri plus Saturday work; Ermco's Google Data Center in Mooresville is moving wire on 5-10's and an 8-hour Saturday. Hospital work at IU with AC and OSHA 10 is also live. This is the Midwest's cleanest board right now.

LU-430 Racine, WI has only 11 calls but they're all money: Gerdes, Pieper, Staff Electric, and Kelso Burnett are all staffing the Microsoft Mount Pleasant project at the full $62.51/hr scale, requiring Wisconsin inside JW or reciprocal cards. Long sleeves and safety shoes non-negotiable. This is a short-term sprint but the pay is top-tier for the region.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND shows 23 calls with Parsons Electric dominating the Polaris Forge Data Center board (35 + 24 Inside JW spots). Base is $44.80/hr but the real move is the $100/day non-taxed per diem for 100+ mile GPS radius, plus $100 Mon-Fri and $150 Sat/Sun incentive on top. That's 6-10's schedule with actual money in pocket. Hunt Electric's Grand Forks Air Base work is slower but cleaner hours (4-10's Fri OT only).

LU-613 Atlanta, GA has 39 calls across Inglett & Stubbs, Miller, and Beltline. The Wellstar Paulding Hospital job pays $40/hr on 4-10's + optional Saturday; Plant Yates in Newnan runs $42.18/hr under SCMMA on 5-10's. Book depth is real—232 hands competing—but the QTS Fayette datacenter work with Miller (cable splicer interview required) pays $40.90/hr on cable work.

LU-26 Washington, DC has 38 calls with FIS paying $125/day per diem on VA Data Centers (Sterling, Loudoun County), M-F 8's with 2 hours daily OT plus Saturday. JE Richards is running dual data center projects in Manassas and Sterling, same OT structure. Valid Electric's chiller plant is new construction pre-hire drug screen.

Data center work is eating the entire board—917 calls, nearly 3,500 hands needed, and 70% of premium work is infrastructure buildout in the upper Midwest and Southeast. Follow the megawatt.

Trampin the IBEW Lineman Locals — Monday, July 13, 2026

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND is printing money right now — 25 calls with Parsons Electric hammering the Polaris Forge Data Center in Harwood on $44.80/hr inside work, but the real play is the $100–$150/day incentive (non-taxed per diem) stacked on 6-10s, plus another $100 bonus for Mon-Fri. Hunt Electric's got 10 more at the Air Base. Book's only showing ~143 hands against 25 calls — that's movement.

LU-71 Columbus, OH remains the heavyweight. New River Electric Corp. is running 37 Journeyman Lineman on distribution rebuilds across Southern Ohio at top scale $61.44/hr, with $125/night PD and a $100 bonus. CDL required. JJM Power's Chillicothe distro crew (4-10s, South Central territory) is legit work. Board shows 34 calls, only ~109 hands sitting — solid ratio.

LU-583 El Paso, TX has gotten tighter but pays. Summit Line and Cupertino Electric are both at $55/hr on projects requiring the NM State JIW License (EE98J). Rosendin's slightly under at $40 + $15 incentive if you stack 90% of 60 hours weekly. All OT double time. 10 calls across a thin bench of ~114 hands.

LU-291 Boise, ID shows $65.33/hr non-PLA work at Lea Electric (MEWP cert required, Ref#8647), but the PLA jobs at Micron are paying $45.33 + $170/day PD. Quality's St. Luke's Tower is $55.33. Nine calls, shallow bench at ~24 hands.

LU-1547 Anchorage, AK is a trap — 39 calls posted but nearly all "Other" classifications: laborer gigs, tree work, and telecom at $45.31/hr max. Real lineman work is sparse here.

Data center buildout is absorbing hands across the upper Midwest and Southwest; distribution work and transmission rebuilds in the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic remain the steadiest long-haul play.
