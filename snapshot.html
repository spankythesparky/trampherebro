Trampin the IBEW Indoor Locals — Tuesday, July 14, 2026

LU-481 Indianapolis, IN is running hot on two massive data center builds. Meade Electric's Meta Data Center play in Lebanon hits $53.80/hr (10% above scale) on 5-12s Monday-Friday plus Saturday 10s — that's real money for a sustained grind. Ermco's Google gig in Mooresville runs 5-10s with Saturday 8s, straight outside new construction. Book depth sits around 188 hands for 43 calls; you're not sitting long here.

The premium is clean: JW wage plus $10/hr base increase with all three major contractors naming it explicitly. You're looking 50-60 hour weeks, valid WI license required, drug test mandatory. 203 hands on 38 calls means the board's moving steady but not desperate.

LU-26 Washington, DC leans heavy on Sterling data center work — JE Richards and FIS both pulling crews for active builds with planned overtime. Aarow's Nolan Building in Springfield offers a break from data center monotony (new construction, EMT work), and per diem sits at $125/day with FIS. 40 calls, 185 hands; solid depth.

LU-481 Indianapolis and LU-481 Indianapolis both pulling from the same Meta/Google infrastructure sprint — the data center boom is real and sustained. LU-430 Racine, WI deserves mention: Microsoft Mount Pleasant paying $62.51/hr across Gerdes, Pieper, and Staff Electric, all starting late June into July. Wisconsin license or reciprocal required.

LU-1426 Grand Forks, ND offers the per diem angle: Parsons Electric's Polaris Forge pull pays $44.80/hr base plus $100/day non-taxed (GPS radius 100+ miles) on 6-10s with $100-150 incentive spreads. Book's thin at 93 hands for 23 calls, but the dollar per diem is a workaround for take-home math.

Data centers are absorbing hands faster than traditional construction; 961 calls across 129 locals means work's everywhere, but the premium hours and sustained schedules are stacking in the Midwest and mid-Atlantic data center corridor.
